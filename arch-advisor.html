Famous Examples
Real-World Decisions what the industry chose — and why
Chose Monolith / Modular Monolith
MONOLITH
Basecamp (37signals)
Intentionally kept a Rails monolith. Small team, high developer productivity, rejected microservices complexity. Powers a profitable multi-million user product.
MONOLITH
Stack Overflow
Runs on a few powerful servers with a .NET monolith. Handles millions of requests/day with extreme efficiency. Proof that hardware can outperform distributed complexity.
REVERTED TO MONO
Segment
Migrated from monolith → 140 microservices → back to a monolith ("Centrifuge"). Found the overhead of 140 services worse than the original problem. Classic over-engineering case.
REVERTED TO MONO
Prime Video (Amazon)
Their video monitoring tool was microservices-first. Migrating to a monolith reduced infrastructure cost by 90% and improved performance. Made waves in 2023.
MODULAR MONO
Shopify
A famously large Rails monolith ("Shopify Core"). Instead of microservices, they invested in component isolation within the monolith and strict module boundaries.
Chose Microservices
MICROSERVICES
Netflix
Pioneered microservices after a 2008 database corruption took them offline. Moved 100% to AWS with 700+ services. Invented Hystrix, Eureka, Zuul — the microservices toolchain.
MICROSERVICES
Amazon
Jeff Bezos' "Two-Pizza Teams" mandate in 2002 forced service boundaries. Each team owns their API. Enabled AWS itself — S3, EC2, etc. are each independent services.
MICROSERVICES
Uber
Scaled from a monolith to 2,200+ microservices to support global operations. Different teams own ride-hailing, payments, maps, surge pricing, etc. independently.
MIGRATED FROM MONO
Twitter / X
Started as a Ruby on Rails monolith ("the Fail Whale" era). Migrated critical paths (timelines, tweets) to JVM microservices to handle 500M tweets/day at scale.
MICROSERVICES
Spotify
Organized around "squads" owning their own services (playlists, search, recommendations, payments). Each squad deploys independently — enabling 10+ releases per day per squad.
